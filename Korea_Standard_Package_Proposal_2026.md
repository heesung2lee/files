## Market Context

While the luxury tier targets top-5% income travelers, this standard tier serves the **broad middle-class Western tourist** — the 80% who want authentic Korea without private drivers, Michelin stars, or 5-star hotels. These packages prioritize:

- **Value density**: maximum experiences per dollar spent
- **Authenticity**: where Koreans actually eat, shop, and hang out
- **Comfort without excess**: clean, well-located 3–4 star hotels
- **Small group dynamics**: 8–12 people, not 40-person bus tours
- **Guided independence**: structured mornings, free afternoons

## Package A-S: "Seoul in a Flash" — 4 Days / 3 Nights

### Price: **$1,180 per person** (twin share)

### Accommodation
**Hotel Ibis Ambassador Insadong** or **Lotte City Hotel Myeongdong** (3.5★)
— Clean, modern, perfect location. Korean power outlets, WiFi, breakfast included.

### Day-by-Day Itinerary

#### Day 1 — Arrival & First Taste of Seoul
- **Airport:** Self-transfer via **AREX Express train** from Incheon → Seoul Station (43 min, $8, ticket provided in welcome packet). Or taxi ($55, optional add-on)
- **3:00 PM:** Hotel check-in. Welcome packet includes: T-money card (pre-loaded 20,000 KRW), subway map, itinerary booklet, WiFi egg pickup info
- **5:00 PM:** **Welcome meetup** at hotel lobby. Guide introduction, group orientation. Hand out contact cards, KakaoTalk group chat created.
- **6:30 PM:** **Welcome dinner** at **Myeongdong Kyoja** — Seoul's most famous kalguksu (handmade noodle soup) and mandu (dumplings). A Seoul institution since 1966. ($10/person, included)
- **8:00 PM:** **Myeongdong walking tour** — street food crawl (guide-led). Try: tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), hotteok (sweet pancake), egg bread, tornado potato. Street food budget included ($10/person)
- **10:00 PM:** Optional: **N Seoul Tower night view** (cable car $14 round-trip, self-guided)

#### Day 2 — Royal Seoul & Traditional Korea
- **8:30 AM:** Hotel breakfast
- **9:30 AM:** **Gyeongbokgung Palace** — guided tour (2 hrs). Catch the 10:00 AM Changing of the Royal Guard ceremony. **Hanbok rental** included — wear traditional Korean dress for the palace (makes you feel the part, great photos). ($15/person hanbok, included)
- **12:00 PM:** **Tosokchon Samgyetang** — the most famous samgyetang (ginseng chicken soup) restaurant in Seoul. No reservation system — guide has pre-lined up. ($15/person, included)
- **1:30 PM:** **Bukchon Hanok Village** — walking tour through 600-year-old traditional Korean houses. Best photo spots, working galleries, hidden tea houses. Free entry.
- **3:00 PM:** **Insadong** — art street. Ssamziegil spiral building, traditional craft shops, **free time** (1.5 hrs). Tea at a traditional tea house (own cost, $5–8)
- **5:00 PM:** **Ikseon-dong** — Seoul's trendiest hanok alley. Vintage shops, concept cafes, photo zones. Guide shows favorites, then free exploration.
- **7:00 PM:** **Dinner at a local Korean BBQ** — not tourist-trap. Guide's favorite neighborhood spot in Jongno. Samgyeopsal (pork belly) + soju. ($18/person, included)
- **9:00 PM:** Optional: **Noraebang (karaoke)** with the group ($8/person, 2 hrs)

#### Day 3 — DMZ & Hongdae Night
- **7:00 AM:** Early pickup. **Group DMZ half-day tour** (shared bus, 25–30 people). Standard DMZ itinerary:
  - Imjingak Peace Park
  - 3rd Infiltration Tunnel (steep walk down — wear comfortable shoes)
  - Dora Observatory (binoculars available, 500 KRW)
  - Dorasan Station
  - DMZ souvenir shop (North Korean currency, ginseng wine available)
  - ($45/person tour fee, included)
- **1:00 PM:** Return to Seoul. **Lunch near Hongdae** — budget Korean stew (jjigae) or bibimbap ($8/person, included)
- **2:30 PM:** Free afternoon. Options (self-guided):
  - **Hongdae street culture** — busking, art market (free, Saturdays only)
  - **Trickeye Museum** or **Love Museum** ($12, optional)
  - **Kakao Friends / Line Friends** flagship stores
  - Cafe hopping — themed cafes (sheep cafe, meerkat cafe, raccoon cafe, $8–12 entrance + drink)
- **7:00 PM:** **Hongdae dinner** — Korean fried chicken + beer (chimaek). At a legendary local chain. ($14/person, included)
- **9:00 PM:** **Hongdae club street** — guide-led club crawl. Entry fees covered for 2 clubs. Drinks on your own. Seoul's electric nightlife, dancing until dawn optional.

#### Day 4 — Markets & Departure
- **8:30 AM:** Hotel breakfast + checkout (luggage storage at hotel)
- **9:30 AM:** **Gwangjang Market** — Korea's oldest traditional market. Guided food crawl:
  - Bindaetteok (mung bean pancake) at the famous Netflix stall
  - Mayak gimbap (addictive mini seaweed rolls)
  - Yukhoe (Korean steak tartare) — optional, adventurous eaters only
  - Makgeolli (rice wine) tasting
  - ($12/person food budget, included)
- **11:30 AM:** **DDP (Dongdaemun Design Plaza)** — Zaha Hadid's masterpiece. Walk the futuristic curves, LED rose garden. Free entry. Photo time.
- **12:30 PM:** Free time / last shopping: Dongdaemun malls or Myeongdong. Guide provides directions and recommendations.
- **3:00 PM:** **Farewell meetup** — group gathers at hotel. Swap photos, KakaoTalk group stays open. Departure gift: small K-beauty sample set.
- **Self-transfer to airport** via AREX train or airport bus

## Package B-S: "Korean Discovery" — 7 Days / 6 Nights

### Price: **$2,180 per person** (twin share)
Single supplement: +$580
Group size: 8–12 travelers

### Route: Seoul (3N) → Gyeongju (1N) → Busan (2N)

### Accommodation
- **Seoul:** Ibis Ambassador Insadong or Holiday Inn Express Hongdae (3.5★)
- **Gyeongju:** Gyeongju Commodore Hotel or GG Tourist Hotel (3★, traditional Korean-style rooms available)
- **Busan:** Toyoko Inn Busan Station or Ibis Ambassador Haeundae (3.5★)

### Day-by-Day Itinerary

#### Day 1 — Arrival & Seoul Orientation
- AREX train self-transfer to hotel
- **3:00 PM:** Hotel check-in + welcome packet
- **5:00 PM:** **Welcome meetup** + group orientation
- **6:00 PM:** **Cheonggyecheon Stream walk** — from Gwanghwamun to Dongdaemun. Guide shares Seoul's urban renewal story.
- **7:00 PM:** **Welcome dinner** at **Gwangjang Market** — a proper market feast. The Netflix-famous knife-cut noodle stall, bindaetteok, mayak gimbap. ($12/person)
- **8:30 PM:** **DDP night photo session** — LED rose garden, curved architecture lit up. Guide is a skilled photographer — group and individual shots.

#### Day 2 — Royal Seoul (Guided Full Day)
- **8:00 AM:** Hotel breakfast
- **9:00 AM:** **Gyeongbokgung Palace** — with hanbok rental. Changing of the Guard at 10 AM.
- **11:30 AM:** **Bukchon Hanok Village** — walking tour. Hidden alleyways, photo spots overlooking the tiled roofs toward Namsan Tower.
- **12:30 PM:** **Lunch at Insadong** — traditional bibimbap in a stone pot (dolsot bibimbap). The rice crackles against the hot stone. ($10/person)
- **1:30 PM:** **Insadong free time** — art galleries, Ssamziegil, traditional tea houses
- **3:00 PM:** **Jogyesa Temple** — main Buddhist temple in Seoul. Colorful lanterns, 500-year-old lacebark pine tree. Free entry. Lotus lantern making if available (seasonal).
- **5:00 PM:** **Ikseon-dong hanok alley** — trendy cafes, vintage shops. Guide's favorite: **Cheongsudang** — a hanok bakery cafe built over a koi pond. ($6 for coffee/dessert, own cost)
- **7:00 PM:** **Korean BBQ dinner** — local spot, not tourist. Pork belly + soju. ($18/person)
- **9:00 PM:** Optional **noraebang** with group ($8/person)

#### Day 3 — DMZ & Hongdae (Guided Morning, Free Afternoon/Evening)
- **7:00 AM:** Group DMZ half-day tour (as in A-S, $45 included)
- **1:00 PM:** Return to Seoul. Lunch near Hongdae ($8 included)
- **2:30 PM:** **Hongdae free afternoon.** Guide provides a map of:
  - Street busking zones (Saturdays best)
  - Concept cafes (sheep, meerkat, raccoon, board game)
  - Vintage clothing shops (incredible deals: $5–15 for quality pieces)
  - Art supply stores (Homi) — amazing stationery
  - Kakao Friends / Line Friends flagship
- **7:00 PM:** **Group Korean fried chicken dinner** ($14/person)
- **9:00 PM:** **Club crawl** (2 clubs included, drinks on own)

#### Day 4 — Seoul → Gyeongju (Travel Day)
- **7:30 AM:** Hotel checkout. Breakfast.
- **8:30 AM:** **KTX train Seoul → Singyeongju** (2 hrs 10 min, economy class). Group seats together. Guide distributes **Hwangnam bread** (Gyeongju's famous red bean pastry) as a train snack. ($35 KTX fare, included)
- **11:00 AM:** Arrive Gyeongju. Private minibus for the day (not shared public bus — included).
- **11:30 AM:** **Bulguksa Temple** (UNESCO) — Silla Dynasty masterpiece. The stone pagodas (Dabotap and Seokgatap) are Korea's National Treasures #20 and #21. Guide explains the intricate Buddhist symbolism. ($5 entry, included)
- **1:00 PM:** **Lunch** at a local restaurant near Bulguksa — **sanchae bibimbap** (mountain vegetable bibimbap) or **haemul pajeon** (seafood pancake). Gyeongju's mountain herbs are famous. ($10/person)
- **2:30 PM:** **Seokguram Grotto** — 8th-century granite Buddha in a mountain shrine overlooking the East Sea. The smile of the Buddha at dawn is legendary — we visit in afternoon but the artistry is timeless. ($5 entry, included)
- **4:00 PM:** Hotel check-in
- **5:00 PM:** **Daereungwon Tomb Complex** — giant grassy mounds, Silla royal tombs. **Cheonmachong** (Heavenly Horse Tomb) — you can enter one tomb, see a gold crown replica in its original burial position. ($3 entry, included)
- **6:30 PM:** **Cheomseongdae Observatory** — 7th-century stone observatory, oldest surviving in Asia. Beautiful in the golden hour light.
- **7:30 PM:** **Dinner at Gyeongju's Hwangnam Bread Street area** — **ssambap** (leaf-wrapped rice set with 10+ banchan side dishes). The local specialty. ($12/person)
- **8:30 PM:** **Donggung Palace & Wolji Pond** — illuminated at night. The reflection of the restored Silla palace in the pond is one of Korea's most photographed scenes. Guide helps with night photography settings. ($3 entry, included)

#### Day 5 — Gyeongju → Busan (Travel Day)
- **8:00 AM:** Hotel breakfast
- **9:00 AM:** **Morning option: Golgulsa Temple** — Sunmudo (Korean Buddhist martial arts) demonstration. Active travelers can try basic forms. Or sleep in and explore the hotel area. ($5 entry)
- **10:30 AM:** **Mini-bus transfer to Busan** (80 min)
- **12:00 PM:** Hotel check-in (Haeundae area)
- **12:30 PM:** **Lunch at Millak-dong Raw Fish Center** — Busan's famous live fish market. Group selects fish from the tank, prepared as sashimi (hoe) on the upper floor. Fish stew (maeuntang) from the bones. A Busan essential experience. ($18/person)
- **2:00 PM:** **Haedong Yonggungsa Temple** — rare seaside Buddhist temple dramatically set on coastal cliffs. Stunning ocean views from the temple grounds. Photo highlight of the trip. Free entry.
- **4:00 PM:** **Haeundae Beach** — Korea's most famous beach. Walk the white sand, dip your feet in the East Sea. **Haeundae Blueline Park** — optionally do the Beach Train or Sky Capsule along the coast ($5–12, optional, guide helps book).
- **6:30 PM:** **Gwangalli Beach sunset** — Gwangan Bridge begins its LED light show at sunset. Beachfront convenience store beer + ramyeon (Korean ritual: "beach maekju"). Dinner: **local dwaeji gukbap** (pork soup rice) — Busan's working-class comfort food. Rich, hearty, perfect after a long day. ($8/person)
- **8:30 PM:** Free evening — explore Gwangalli's beachfront bars and cafes

#### Day 6 — Busan Deep Dive
- **8:00 AM:** Hotel breakfast
- **9:00 AM:** **Gamcheon Culture Village** — the "Machu Picchu of Busan." Colorful hillside village, street art murals, the Little Prince statue viewpoint. Guide knows the fastest routes to the best photo spots. Free entry. ($2 optional village map for stamp collection)
- **11:30 AM:** **Jagalchi Fish Market** — Korea's largest seafood market. The wet market on the ground floor is a sensory explosion. Try **sannakji** (live octopus) if brave — the tentacles still squirm on the plate. Group buys a platter to share. ($10/person for shared seafood)
- **1:00 PM:** **Lunch at Jagalchi** — grilled fish set on the 2F eating area. Fresh from the tank below. ($12/person)
- **2:30 PM:** **BIFF Square** — Busan International Film Festival zone. Handprints of famous Asian directors. Street food stalls: ssiat hotteok (seed-filled sweet pancake, Busan's specialty). ($3)
- **3:30 PM:** **Yongdusan Park & Busan Tower** — panoramic city and harbor views from the observation deck. ($9, optional)
- **5:00 PM:** Free time — guide suggestions:
  - **Songdo Cloud Trails** — glass walkway over the ocean (free)
  - **Huinyeoul Culture Village** — smaller, less touristy version of Gamcheon
  - Shopping at **Lotte Department Store Gwangbok** (rooftop garden with ocean view)
- **7:00 PM:** **Farewell group dinner** — **Samgyeopsal or Hanwoo BBQ** in Seomyeon (Busan's food central). The group's final meal together. Soju/makgeolli toast to close the trip. ($15/person)
- **9:00 PM:** Optional: **Seomyeon nightlife** — the district has Seoul-level energy. Karaoke, bars, late-night food. Guide navigates and makes sure everyone gets back safely.

#### Day 7 — Departure
- **Morning:** Hotel breakfast. Checkout.
- **Free time until departure.** Options based on flight time:
  - Morning flight: Self-transfer to **Gimhae International Airport** (light rail from Haeundae, 40 min, $3)
  - Afternoon/evening flight: KTX Busan → Seoul (2.5 hrs), then AREX to Incheon Airport. Total: $45, about 4 hours door-to-door. Guide helps time it perfectly.
- **Departure gift:** K-beauty sample set, group photo from the trip (digital)
- **KakaoTalk group stays open** — many travelers continue sharing Korea tips and meet up again

## Package C-S: "Ultimate Korea" — 13 Days / 12 Nights

### Price: **$3,980 per person** (twin share)
Single supplement: +$980
Group size: 8–12 travelers

### Route: Seoul (4N) → Andong (1N) → Gyeongju (2N) → Busan (2N) → Jeju Island (3N)

### Accommodation
- **Seoul:** Lotte City Hotel Myeongdong or Nine Tree Premier Insadong (3.5★)
- **Andong:** Andong Park Hotel or traditional hanok guesthouse (rustic but authentic, ondol floor sleeping)
- **Gyeongju:** Commodore Hotel or Gyeongju Hanok Sodamjeong (hanok option)
- **Busan:** Ibis Ambassador Haeundae or Toyoko Inn Seomyeon (3.5★)
- **Jeju:** Lotte City Hotel Jeju or Ramada Plaza Jeju Ocean Front (4★)

### Day-by-Day Itinerary

#### Day 1 — Arrival & Welcome to Seoul
- Self-transfer via AREX to hotel (ticket provided)
- **3:00 PM:** Check-in + welcome packet
- **5:00 PM:** Group welcome meetup
- **6:00 PM:** **Cheonggyecheon Stream** walk + orientation
- **7:00 PM:** **Welcome dinner** at a local **samgyeopsal** restaurant. Ice-breaking with soju and beer. Guide teaches proper Korean drinking etiquette. ($18/person)
- **9:00 PM:** **Gwangjang Market** late-night food crawl or rest at hotel

#### Day 2 — Royal Seoul
_(Same structure as B-S Day 2 but slightly more relaxed pace)_
- Gyeongbokgung Palace + hanbok
- Tosokchon Samgyetang lunch
- Bukchon Hanok Village
- Insadong free time
- Jogyesa Temple
- Dinner: Korean BBQ or **sundubu jjigae** (soft tofu stew) — the dish that put Korea's comfort food on the global map. ($12/person)

#### Day 3 — DMZ & K-Culture
- Morning: Standard DMZ group tour ($45)
- Lunch near Hongdae
- **Afternoon option (choose one, included):**
  - **K-pop dance class** — 1-hour group session at a real dance studio. Learn a chorus from a current hit song
  - **Korean cooking class** — 2-hour session. Make kimchi jjigae + japchae + pajeon. Eat your creations.
- Dinner: Korean fried chicken in Hongdae
- Evening: Club crawl (2 clubs included)

#### Day 4 — Modern Seoul & Free Exploration
- **9:00 AM:** **DDP + Dongdaemun morning walk**
- **10:00 AM:** **Starfield COEX Library** — the Instagram-famous giant bookshelf. Free. **Gangnam Style statue** outside COEX. **Bongeunsa Temple** across the street — urban Buddhist temple, striking contrast with COEX's glass towers.
- **12:00 PM:** **Lunch at a Gangnam office-worker favorite** — naengmyeon (cold buckwheat noodles) or kimchi jjigae. Real Seoul lunch culture. ($8/person)
- **2:00 PM:** **Free afternoon.** Guide provides a self-guided map of:
  - **Garosugil** — tree-lined avenue, K-fashion boutiques, flagship stores
  - **Apgujeong Rodeo** — luxury shopping, K-Star Road (GangnamDol bear statues)
  - **Seoul Forest** — urban park, deer enclosure, G-Dragon mural
  - **Itaewon** — international district, craft burger/Korean-Mexican fusion, vintage vinyl shops
  - **War Memorial of Korea** — free, world-class museum. Powerful, balanced narrative.
- **7:00 PM:** **Optional group dinner** (pay on your own). Guide suggests spots based on what you explored. Or use the evening for **jjimjilbang** (Korean spa) — Dragon Hill Spa is open 24 hours ($12, optional)
- **Night:** Free. Your last Seoul night.

#### Day 5 — Seoul → Andong (Scenic Transfer)
- **8:00 AM:** Hotel checkout. Breakfast.
- **9:00 AM:** **Private bus transfer** to Andong (2.5 hrs). Scenic drive through Sobaeksan foothills.
- **11:30 AM:** Arrive Andong. Check into hotel or hanok guesthouse (traditional option: ondol floor sleeping, futon mats — basic but authentic. Some rooms have Western beds. Book early for hanok.)
- **12:00 PM:** **Lunch: Andong Jjimdak** — the city's invention. Braised chicken with glass noodles, potatoes, carrots, in a sweet-spicy soy sauce. At the restaurant street where the dish was born. ($10/person)
- **1:30 PM:** **Hahoe Village** (UNESCO) — a living 600-year-old clan village. People still live here. Thatched-roof houses, Confucian academy, stunning riverside setting under Buyongdae Cliff. ($3 entry)
- **3:00 PM:** **Hahoe Mask Dance performance** — Korea's most treasured folk art. Satirical, comedic, profound. The lion/drunken nobleman/old woman characters. The audience participates at the end. Free performance (tips appreciated).
- **5:00 PM:** **Buyongdae Cliff viewpoint** — cross the river by foot in shallow season or boat, then hike 20 minutes up for the panoramic view of Hahoe Village nestled in the river bend. The iconic photo of Andong.
- **7:00 PM:** **Dinner at a traditional Andong restaurant** — **heotjesabap** (fake ancestral rite meal). A full table spread: jeon (pancakes), japchae, grilled fish, multiple banchan, soup. Andong's ceremonial cuisine. ($12/person)
- **8:30 PM:** **Andong Soju tasting** — Korea's strongest traditional liquor (45% ABV). Visit a 400-year-old brewery. Tasting flight included. ($8, included)
- **Night:** Ondol floor sleeping in hanok — heated from beneath, profoundly cozy. Best sleep of the trip for many.

#### Day 6 — Andong → Gyeongju
- **7:30 AM:** Early breakfast at hanok — rice, soup, banchan. Simple, perfect.
- **8:30 AM:** **Dosan Seowon** — Korea's most beautiful Confucian academy. Situated on a pristine river bend with mountains beyond. Morning mist over the water. Unreal serenity. ($3 entry)
- **10:00 AM:** **Private bus transfer** to Gyeongju (1.5 hrs)
- **11:30 AM:** Hotel check-in
- **12:00 PM:** **Lunch** — Gyeongju specialty ssambap ($10)
- **1:30 PM:** **Gyeongju UNESCO afternoon:**
  - Bulguksa Temple (2 hrs — more time than B-S)
  - Seokguram Grotto
  - Daereungwon Tombs + Cheonmachong
  - Cheomseongdae at golden hour
- **7:00 PM:** **Dinner** — premium **hanwoo BBQ** at a Gyeongju beef village. Three cuts of Korean native beef. ($25/person, the priciest meal in the standard package — special occasion)
- **8:30 PM:** **Donggung Palace & Wolji Pond** illuminated night walk

#### Day 7 — Gyeongju Morning → Busan Afternoon
- **8:00 AM:** Hotel breakfast
- **9:00 AM:** **Morning options (choose):**
  - **Golgulsa Sunmudo** — martial arts demonstration, try basic forms ($5)
  - **Bomun Lake** — leisurely bike ride along the resort lake (bike rental $5/hr)
  - **Yangdong Village** — another UNESCO folk village, less visited than Hahoe
  - **Sleep in** — you're on vacation
- **11:00 AM:** Hotel checkout
- **11:30 AM:** **Bus transfer to Busan** (80 min) with a lunch stop en route at a Korean highway rest stop (hyugeso) — hot meals, cleanest rest stops in the world, unique Korean road trip culture. ($8 lunch budget)
- **2:00 PM:** Hotel check-in (Haeundae area)
- **3:00 PM:** **Haeundae Beach** + **Blueline Park** — Beach Train or Sky Capsule (guide helps book, $5–12 optional)
- **6:00 PM:** **Gwangalli Beach** — dinner at a beachfront seafood restaurant. Grilled fish, clam stew, soju. ($15/person)
- **8:00 PM:** Gwangan Bridge light show + beach walk. Free evening.

#### Day 8 — Busan Full Day
- **8:30 AM:** Hotel breakfast
- **9:30 AM:** **Gamcheon Culture Village** — guide takes the group through the photo route, then free exploration. ($2 optional map)
- **11:30 AM:** **Jagalchi Fish Market** — the full experience. Wet market, live octopus tasting, seafood platter. ($12/person food)
- **1:00 PM:** **Lunch** at Jagalchi 2F — grilled fish set ($12)
- **2:30 PM:** **Haedong Yonggungsa Temple** — seaside temple
- **4:00 PM:** **Free afternoon:** Options —
  - **Songdo Beach + Cloud Trails** (glass walkway, free)
  - **Taejongdae Park** (dramatic cliffs, lighthouse, Danubi train $3, self-transfer via bus)
  - Shopping at **Seomyeon** or **Nampo-dong**
- **7:00 PM:** **Group dinner** — **milmyeon** (Busan's cold wheat noodles, a Korean War-born dish unique to the city) + **dwaeji gukbap** (pork soup rice). Busan soul food. ($10/person)
- **9:00 PM:** Optional: Seomyeon nightlife or hotel rest

#### Day 9 — Busan → Jeju Island
- **7:30 AM:** Hotel checkout. Early breakfast.
- **8:30 AM:** Transfer to **Gimhae Airport** (light rail, $3)
- **10:00 AM:** **Flight to Jeju** (1 hr). Budget airline (Jeju Air / Jin Air / T'way). 15kg check-in included. Group sits together. ($55–75 flight, included)
- **11:30 AM:** Arrive Jeju. Private minibus for island exploration (included all 3 days — public transit in Jeju is sparse).
- **12:30 PM:** **Lunch: Jeju Black Pork BBQ** — the island's most famous dish. Thick-cut, grilled over charcoal. At the original **Heukdonga** or a local favorite. ($15/person)
- **2:00 PM:** Hotel check-in (Seogwipo area recommended — more scenic)
- **3:00 PM:** **East Coast Jeju:**
  - **Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak)** — UNESCO. The massive volcanic tuff cone rising from the sea. 30-minute hike to the crater rim. Panoramic ocean views. ($3 entry)
  - **Haenyeo (Women Divers) performance** — watch Jeju's legendary free-diving grandmothers (60–80+ years old) harvest seafood from the ocean floor with no oxygen tanks. UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. Profound. Free (buy some fresh-caught seafood, $5)
- **7:00 PM:** **Seogwipo Maeil Olle Market** — Jeju's best traditional market. Food crawl: hallabong (Jeju tangerine) juice, peanut ice cream, black pork skewers, Jeju mandarin makgeolli. ($10 food budget)
- **Night:** Free. Seogwipo harbor walk.

### Transport Tips

- **T-money card:** Use on all subways, buses, taxis, and convenience stores across Korea. Pre-loaded. Tap on AND off buses (not subways). Card works in Seoul, Busan, Jeju, Gyeongju.
- **KakaoTaxi:** The Uber of Korea. Download before arrival. English interface available. Works everywhere. Pay via T-money or registered card.
- **Subway:** Seoul's system is world-class. English signage everywhere. Apps: **KakaoMetro** or **Naver Map**. Google Maps does NOT have walking/driving directions in Korea (legal restriction). Install Naver Map or KakaoMap before arrival.
- **KTX:** Korea's bullet train. 305 km/h. Punctual to the second. Economy class is comfortable with generous legroom. No need for first class. Book via **Korail** app or let the guide handle it.
- **Flights to Jeju:** The world's busiest air route (Seoul–Jeju). 180+ flights daily. Budget carriers (Jeju Air, Jin Air, T'way, Air Busan) all safe, modern, and punctual. 15kg check-in included. 1-hour flight.

### Korean Spa (Jjimjilbang) 101

The Korean spa is a cultural experience, not just a bath. Protocol:
1. Pay entry ($8–12). Receive pajamas + towel.
2. Gender-segregated bath area: shower thoroughly, soak in hot tubs (40°C), cold plunge, steam room. **You must be fully nude** in the wet area. This is normal. No one stares.
3. Change into pajamas. Co-ed common area: heated floors, various sauna rooms (salt, clay, charcoal, ice, jade), snack bar, nap zone, massage chairs.
4. You can sleep here overnight. Many travelers do this on their last night to save a hotel night (and for the experience).
5. The **seshin (body scrub)** is legendary: a professional ddumiri scrubs every inch of your skin. You will shed rolls of dead skin you didn't know you had. Intense but transformative. ($25–40 extra)

Recommended: **Dragon Hill Spa** (Yongsan, Seoul) — most foreigner-friendly, largest facility. **Spa Land** (Centum City, Busan) — 18 themed sauna rooms. Open 6 AM–midnight (not 24 hrs).

### Safety & Practicalities

- **Korea is extremely safe.** Violent crime is rare. Theft is uncommon. Solo female travelers consistently rate Korea #1 in Asia for safety.
- **Lost & Found culture:** Koreans routinely turn in lost items. If you leave something on a bus or taxi, it's likely at the lost and found. Guide helps recover.
- **Emergency numbers:** 112 (police), 119 (ambulance/fire). 1330 (Tourist Help Line — English, 24/7). 1339 (Medical Emergency — English available).
- **Pharmacies:** Over-the-counter medicine is limited in Korea compared to Western countries. Bring your preferred painkillers, antihistamines, cold medicine.
- **Tap water:** Safe to drink but most Koreans don't. Bottled water is cheap ($0.60/500ml).
- **Toilets:** Western-style toilets are common in cities. Carry tissue — some older public restrooms may not have it. Korean bidet toilets are glorious.
- **Power:** 220V, Type C/F plugs (two round pins). US devices need an adapter (not just a converter — check voltage compatibility). Hotel front desks usually have adapters.

### Money-Saving Tips

1. **Street food dinners:** An entire dinner of street food at Gwangjang or Myeongdong costs $6–10. Delicious, fast, cultural.
2. **Convenience store meals:** Korean convenience stores (CU, GS25, 7-Eleven) have legitimately good food — kimbap ($2), ramyeon with hot water dispenser ($1), triangle rice balls ($1), Korean "lunch boxes" ($4–6). Perfect for a quick breakfast or late-night snack.
3. **Free attractions:** Gyeongbokgung is free if you wear hanbok. Jogyesa Temple, Cheonggyecheon, DDP, COEX Library, Haeundae Beach, Gamcheon Village — all free.
4. **Subway > taxi:** Seoul subway is cleaner, faster during rush hour, and costs $1–2 per ride. Taxi minimum is $3.80.
5. **Convenience store beer:** Drinking in public is legal in Korea. A convenience store beer on the Han River or at the beach is a cherished Korean ritual. Cans are $1.50–2.50.
6. **Duty-free shopping:** If you want Korean cosmetics or luxury goods, buy at Lotte/Shinsegae Duty Free (Myeongdong). Present passport + flight info. Cheaper than retail. Collect at the airport.
