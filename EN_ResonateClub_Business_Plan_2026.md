## 1. Executive Summary

Resonate Club is a **premium boutique golf tour operator** connecting Korea and California. Founded by a Korean-American with F-4 (Overseas Korean) visa status, the agency operates as a lean 1-person business running **bidirectional tour products**: California golf tours for Korean outbound travelers, and Korea golf tours for international inbound guests.

The initial strategy focuses on **small private groups (4–8 guests)** with simple, repeatable route packages — minimizing fixed costs while targeting rapid break-even.

## 2. Market Opportunity

### 2.1 Korea's Outbound Golf Boom

- Korean golf population: **~6 million** (1 in 8 Koreans)
- Domestic green fees rising steadily → surge in **overseas golf travel demand**
- Approx. **500,000 Korean golf travelers/year** (2024 estimate)
- Current popular destinations: Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Japan
- **California is emerging** as a premium alternative — world-class courses, ideal climate, and the allure of West Coast lifestyle

### 2.2 California's Golf Differentiation

- **Sandpiper GC** (Santa Barbara): Golf Digest Top 50, oceanfront championship course, PGA Championship history
- **Trump National LA** (Rancho Palos Verdes): Cliffside fairways overlooking the Pacific, globally recognized design
- **Hidden Valley GC** (Norco): Rolling hills layout, exceptional value, easy inland access
- 16-hour time difference advantage → **52-week golf climate** (peak March–November)
- Currency edge: Dollar-denominated premium experience retains aspirational value

### 2.3 Korea Inbound Golf Growth

- **Ananti Namhae CC**: Hallyeohaesang National Park views, PGA-style seaside design, bucket-list destination for international golfers
- K-drama / Hallyu proximity → golf + culture package appeal
- Rising demand from Japanese, Chinese, and Southeast Asian golf travelers
- Ananti resort integration → accommodation + golf + spa in one destination

## 5. Pricing Strategy

### 5.1 Green Fee Policy

Per founder directive, **weekend prime-time green fees are passed through at cost** — no markup on the golf itself. The 25% margin is applied to accommodation, dining, transportation, and guide services where value-add curation justifies the premium.

Future margin expansion: group-rate negotiations with courses (10–20% off rack rates) once operating volume is established.

## 6. Operations Plan

- **F-4 (Overseas Korean) Visa**: Unrestricted employment, business registration permitted, indefinite stay in Korea
- **Tourism Promotion Act**: Travel agency registration with Seoul Metropolitan Government — ~₩500,000 one-time fee

## 9. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Currency fluctuation (KRW/USD) | Cost increase | KRW-based pricing adjustments, pre-contract in USD-weak periods |
| Airfare spikes | Customer attrition | Early-bird ticketing, fuel surcharge monitoring |
| Golf course unavailability | Schedule disruption | Maintain backup course list (Cross Creek, GC of California, The Crossings) |
| Natural disaster / pandemic | Full suspension | Clear cancellation/refund policy, mandatory travel insurance inclusion |
| Competitive entry | Margin pressure | Private curation differentiation, loyalty-based repeat customer focus |
